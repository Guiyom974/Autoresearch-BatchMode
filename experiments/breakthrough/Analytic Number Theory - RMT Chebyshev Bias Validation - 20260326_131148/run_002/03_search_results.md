### 1. How to Write the Limitations of a Study (Wordvice)
This article emphasizes that researchers should not just list limitations but explain how they impact the research findings. Common limitations include limited access to data, time constraints, and researcher bias. The author suggests organizing the limitations section into three steps: identifying the limitation, explaining its impact, and proposing directions for future studies or alternative methodologies to "fill in the gaps." [[1]](https://blog.wordvice.com/how-to-present-study-limitations-and-alternatives/)
**Source:** [Wordvice - Limitations of the Study](https://blog.wordvice.com/how-to-present-study-limitations-and-alternatives/)

### 2. Characteristics and Types of a Research Problem (Researcher.life)
A research problem is defined as a gap in existing knowledge or a real-world challenge. The article highlights that practical research problems face limitations due to constraints in methodology, resources, or the complexity of the problem itself. It also notes that research problems can evolve during the investigation process as new complexities or limitations arise, requiring researchers to tweak their scope to remain relevant. [[2]](https://researcher.life/blog/article/what-is-a-research-problem-types-and-examples/)
**Source:** [Researcher.life - What is a Research Problem?](https://researcher.life/blog/article/what-is-a-research-problem-types-and-examples/)

### 3. How to Pick a Research Problem That Actually Matters (Medium)
Lennart Nacke advises junior researchers to "hunt the limitations sections" of existing papers to find valuable research problems. Authors explicitly state future directions and gaps in these sections, which typically fall into four categories: evidence gaps, methodological gaps, population gaps, and practical knowledge gaps. He suggests filtering potential problems by significance, feasibility (resource limitations), and sustainability. [[3]](https://acagamic.medium.com/how-to-pick-a-research-problem-that-actually-matters-aa0d55ecaffa)
**Source:** [Medium - How to pick a research problem that actually matters](https://acagamic.medium.com/how-to-pick-a-research-problem-that-actually-matters-aa0d55ecaffa)

### 4. Identifying and Resolving Research Problems (Formplus)
This guide outlines the challenges researchers face when developing a problem statement, such as overcomplicating the problem, getting lost in the literature, and procrastination. A major challenge highlighted is ensuring the problem aligns with the researcher's skills and resources; insufficient resources and experience are the primary reasons researchers abandon projects or face rejection. [[4]](https://www.formpl.us/blog/defining-a-research-problem-how-to-identify-and-resolve-research-problems)
**Source:** [Formplus - Defining a Research Problem](https://www.formpl.us/blog/defining-a-research-problem-how-to-identify-and-resolve-research-problems)

### 5. Limited by our Limitations (NIH / PMC)
This academic paper argues that researchers often limit the impact of their work by providing abbreviated, generic limitations (e.g., "small sample size"). A meaningful presentation of limitations should describe the weakness, explain its implications, provide alternative approaches, and describe steps taken to mitigate it. The authors stress that other researchers use these limitations as the first step in formulating new research questions and shaping future directions. [[5]](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6684501/)
