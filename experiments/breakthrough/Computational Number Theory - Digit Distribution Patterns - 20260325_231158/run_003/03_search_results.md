While it focuses heavily on the statistical modeling of these sequences (using pseudo-Voigt fit functions and log-linear histograms), it highlights the deep inner structures of prime sequences across massive datasets (up to 50 million primes) [[5]](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/377853941_Statistical_Distributions_of_Prime_Number_Gaps).

### 5. Predicting Maximal Gaps in Sets of Primes (MDPI)
This mathematical paper investigates maximal prime gaps—gaps that are strictly greater than any preceding gap. It looks at prime $k$-tuples (clusters of primes with specific patterns) and sequences of primes in specific residue classes. The authors use heuristic formulas to predict the growth trend of maximal gaps, noting that the distribution of rescaled maximal gaps closely follows the Gumbel extreme value distribution [[6]](https://www.mdpi.com/2227-7390/7/5/400).
* **Source:** [MDPI Mathematics](https://www.mdpi.com/2227-7390/7/5/400)

### Query: "KL divergence" OR "Kullback-Leibler" primes composites "digit distribution"
Here is a summary of the search results matching your query. The combination of these specific terms primarily appears in literature concerning **Benford's Law** (the law of anomalous numbers), where **Kullback-Leibler (KL) divergence** is used as a statistical metric to measure how far an empirical **digit distribution** (such as the first digits of primes, composites, or real-world data) deviates from the expected logarithmic distribution.

### 1. Benford's Law: Theory and Applications (Book)
* **Summary:** This comprehensive mathematical text explores the theory behind Benford's Law and the first-digit distribution of various mathematical sequences. It discusses how certain sequences of numbers (which in number theory often include studies on primes and composites) conform to or deviate from Benford's distribution. The book explicitly highlights the use of the **Kullback–Leibler (KL) divergence** and maximum entropy principles to calculate the minimum distance between empirical digit distributions and the ideal Benford limit [[1]](https://dokumen.pub/benfords-law-theory-and-applications-1nbsped-9780691147611.html).
* **Source:** [DOKUMEN.PUB - Benford's Law: Theory and Applications](https://dokumen.pub/benfords-law-theory-and-applications-1nbsped-9780691147611.html)

### 2. A No-Reference Quality Assessment Method for Hyperspectral Sharpened Images via Benford's Law
* **Summary:** This paper applies the concept of digit distribution to image processing. The authors extract the First Digit Distribution (FDD) of hyperspectral images and compare it against the standard Benford's law distribution. They utilize **symmetric Kullback–Leibler (sKL) divergence** to measure the distance between the two probability distributions, proving that the low-frequency FDD of original images closely conforms to Benford's law [[2]](https://www.mdpi.com/2072-4292/16/7/1167).
* **Source:** [MDPI - Remote Sensing](https://www.mdpi.com/2072-4292/16/7/1167)
