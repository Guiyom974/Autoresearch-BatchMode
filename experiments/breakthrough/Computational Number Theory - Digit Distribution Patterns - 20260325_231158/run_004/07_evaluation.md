## Evaluation Summary

**Breakthrough Achieved**: False
**Confidence**: 35.0%

**Summary**:
Primorial bases (especially base-30) show modestly higher KL divergence from uniform n-gram distributions compared to non-primorial bases, but effect sizes are small and no strong structural advantage is demonstrated.

**Next Directions**:
- Extend analysis to higher primorial bases (e.g., base-210, base-2310) where digit exclusion effects are stronger and n-gram biases may be more pronounced
- Compare n-gram distributions of leading digits/prefixes specifically rather than all digit positions, as primorial structure most strongly constrains terminal digits
- Apply mutual information or chi-squared tests instead of KL divergence to better quantify statistical significance of observed biases given the sample size of 78,498 primes
